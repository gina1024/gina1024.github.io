How to Do a Correct Plank

By Gina Solano


Step 1: Forearm & Hand Setup

Do: Elbows under shoulders. Forearms parallel (shoulder-width). Palms flat or soft fists. Fingers spread. Press the floor.

Cue: “Train tracks. Press—don’t sink.”

Avoid: Clasped hands. Elbows caving in.

 

Step 2: Neck & Head Alignment

Do: Keep your neck in line with your spine. Slight chin tuck. Eyes look down between your forearms.

Cue: “Long neck. Look at the mat.”

Avoid: Looking forward or dropping your head.

 

Step 3: Shoulders & Upper Back

Do: Press your forearms into the floor and push the floor away so your shoulder blades spread slightly and stay flat on your ribcage. Keep shoulders down from your ears.

Cue: “Push & spread.”

Avoid: Pinching the shoulder blades together, sinking the chest, or shrugging.

 

Step 4: Core & Hips

Do: Pull ribs toward pelvis, squeeze glutes, and give a slight tailbone tuck. Keep a straight line from shoulders to hips. Think “belly button up.”

Cue: “Zip ribs. Squeeze glutes. Straight line.”

Avoid: Sagging lower back or lifting hips too high.

 

Step 5: Legs & Feet & Final Result

Do: Keep feet hip-width, toes tucked, and press your heels back. Legs straight and active. Ankles stay neutral, feet parallel.

Cue: “Heels back. Feet parallel.”

Avoid: A Feet squeezed together, ankles rolling in/out, or toes turning out.
